natural | BrE ˈnatʃ(ə)r(ə)l, AmE ˈnætʃ(ə)rəl |
	A.adjective
		①(usual, normal) 正常的 ‹reaction, consequence›
			▸ it is natural (for sb) to do sth;
			 （某人）做某事是很正常的
			▸ it is natural that …;
			  …是很正常的
			▸ it is natural that he should want to see his daughter
			  他想见女儿是很自然的
			▸ the natural thing to do would be to protest
			  抗议是理所当然的
			▸ it's only natural
			  这很正常
		②(in accordance with nature) 本能的 ‹urge, behaviour, aversion›;
		   (innate) 天生的 ‹ability, orator, linguist›
			▸ it is natural (for sb/sth) to do sth;
			  （某人/某物）天生会做某事
			▸ it's not natural!
			  这有悖天性！
			▸ sb's natural inclinations
			  某人与生俱来的爱好
			▸ a natural gift for singing
			  歌唱的天赋
		③(unaffected) 自然的
			▸ to be natural with sb;
			  与某人在一起时轻松自如
			▸ try and look more natural
			  尽量表现得再自然一些
		④(not man-made) 自然的 ‹phenomenon, disaster, immunity›;
		   天然的 ‹substance, harbour›; 天生的 ‹blonde, curls, beauty›
			▸ the natural world
			  自然界
			▸ superb natural advantages/features
			  得天独厚的优势/特色
			▸ land in its natural state
			  未开垦的土地
			▸ to die of or from natural causes
			  自然死亡
		⑤(wild) 野生的 ‹food, state›
			▸ natural habitat
			  自然栖息地
			▸ the elephant has no natural predators
			  象没有天敌
		⑥attributive(related by blood) 亲生的 ‹parents, child›
		⑦dated (illegitimate) 私生的 ‹child›
		⑧(unprocessed) 纯天然的 ‹foods, ingredients, medicines›
		⑨Music 本位音的 ‹key, scale›
